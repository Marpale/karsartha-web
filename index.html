Doctrinal rules

  • Only Q1 = BUY zone. Q2 is priced-in growth; Q3 is value-trap watch (cheap but stagnant); Q4 is avoid.
  • Right of safety line ≠ guaranteed profit. A loss-maker on the right is "loss shrinking", not "profitable now". X-axis reads as second-derivative.
  • Left of safety line ≠ guaranteed loss. Mirror: a profitable name on the left is "profit thinning" — forward warning, not current verdict.
  • Red zones (far-left forecast band ≈ X < −15, and deep-bottom strip Y down to ≈ −30) = do-not-place-confidently. Default attribution: lack of circle-of-competence on that name, NOT market mispricing.
  • A good company is not necessarily in a better quadrant (slide 10 doctrine). Quality and Value-Universe position are different statements — MIKA is the canonical counter-example.
  • Coordinates are dynamic. Re-plot quarterly minimum. Sudden corp action (ADRO-style) can shift a dot across quadrants in one quarter.
  • Forecast axis is %-driven, not absolute. SDRA's +14% sits further right than BBNI's +3%, even though absolute IDR delta is smaller.
